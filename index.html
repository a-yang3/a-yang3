About Me
I'm currently a second year studying Cognitive Science at the University of California, Berkeley. My background encompasses neuroscience, design and coding because the intersection of the three makes me really excited. I'm pretty proficient with the Adobe Creative Suite (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere) and wireframing applications (Sketch, Principle). I've also dabbled in Python, HTML, and Swift for iOS development. I love getting my hands on new things and making stuff, some of which you can check out in my portfolio. For fun, I make music with the UC Berkeley Symphony Orchestra and teach as part of the facilitation team for Art 198/"Art for Animation".

Relevant Coursework: The Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs | Methods of Mathematics: Calculus, Statistics, Combinatorics | Introduction to Cognitive Science | General Chemistry | Foundations of Data Science | Brain, Mind, Behavior | Data Structures

WAKE UP CALL - ART 198 | This was a short animatic I made for the UC Berkeley Undergraduate Graphics Group Course: 2D Art for Animators (Art 198). I went through storyboarding and character design processes while learning how to bring my images to life with Photoshop and Premiere. This was my first time deviating from just static drawings, and I enjoyed the class so much that I'm returning as a member of the facilitation team for Fall 2017. Click to watch my animatic.
I work as an undergraduate researcher in the Isacoff Lab, a part of the Molecular/Cell Biology and Helen Willis Neuroscience departments at UC Berkeley. My current project involves in situ hybridization in zebrafish embryos to investigate the role of a specific ion channel protein in neural signaling. Neuroscience research is fascinating to me, and I've learned a lot from the process of succeeding and failing my way through numerous experiments. Click to find out more about my lab.
